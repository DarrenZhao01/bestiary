African Rabies (World War Z Virus)

World War Z (WWZ) by Marc Forster


Summary

World War Z follows the “African rabies” outbreak as it is publicly revealed in China. It escalates towards spreading through black markets and trade. Various social issues stem from the pandemic, with countries like Russia, China, and America altering social policies differently. These alterations initially emphasize maintaining stability over protecting people. Ultimately, various sacrifices are made by both social institutions and individuals, alongside a global effort to save humanity.

Description

“Ignorance was the enemy. Lies and superstition, misinformation, disinformation. Sometimes, no information at all. Ignorance killed billions of people. Ignorance caused the Zombie War. Imagine if we had known then what we know now. Imagine if the undead virus had been as understood as, say, tuberculosis was. Imagine if the world's citizens, or at least those charged with protecting those citizens, had known exactly what they were facing. Ignorance was the real enemy, and cold, hard facts were the weapons” (Whitehead 83)

The virus creates powerful and hyperactive zombies. However, the virus is able to succeed so well because of international connections, alongside governments undermining its existence, and the world consequently being unprepared for it. The virus works on a cellular level to turn people into zombies in seconds. The only way to stop it is sanctioning.

Connection to its Culture

African rabies reflects a fear of nature, hence why it's not perceived as a biological weapon. It shows a fear of the unknown and how nature fights against human impact. Additionally, the disease is booming due to global corruption from ignorance, denial of the disease, and human exploitation, which are all issues relevant in the 21st century.
