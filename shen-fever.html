Shen Fever

Severance by Ling Ma


Summary of Severance

In "Severance," Candace Chen is a routine-driven millennial working in book production in New York City when a mysterious pandemic called Shen Fever begins to spread globally. Unlike typical deadly diseases, this virus causes infected individuals to become trapped in endless loops of familiar tasks—cooking the same meal repeatedly, cleaning the same room obsessively—until they ultimately die. As the world gradually collapses around her, Candace continues working, documenting the pandemic's progression through her job managing Bible production in Chinese factories. Her narrative alternates between two timelines: her life before the pandemic and her post-apocalyptic survival journey. When civilization ultimately crumbles, Candace joins a group of survivors led by Bob, a former IT manager. This ragtag group travels across a devastated America, seeking a potential safe haven. Bob establishes strict rules and hierarchies within the group, ironically mirroring the corporate structures they've left behind. Candace's personal history as a first-generation Chinese-American adds depth to her survival story. Flashbacks reveal her immigrant parents' experiences and her own somewhat detached relationship with her cultural heritage. Her pragmatic, almost emotionally distant approach to the apocalypse sets her apart from typical survivalist protagonists. The group's journey is fraught with challenges: scarce resources, interpersonal conflicts, and the ever-present threat of becoming infected with Shen Fever. Throughout their travels, Candace reflects on her past life, her disconnection from meaningful relationships, and the soul-crushing nature of her pre-pandemic corporate existence. As they search for a potential settlement, the survivors confront the remnants of a world destroyed not just by a virus, but by the repetitive, meaningless routines that defined modern life.

Description

“In its initial stages, Shen Fever is difficult to detect. Early symptoms include memory lapse, headaches, disorientation, shortness of breath, and fatigue. Because these symptoms are often mistaken for the common cold, patients are often unaware they have contracted Shen Fever. They may appear functional and are still able to execute rote, everyday tasks. However, these initial symptoms will worsen” (Ma 14).

Shen Fever is a non-transmitted disease caused by a fungal spore infection. The disease originated in China and caused a worldwide pandemic. The disease is noted to cause the infected to repeat tasks. It also seems to be triggered by waves of nostalgia, usually when people enter a location that has meaning to their past.

Connection to its Culture

Shen Fever has specific characteristics that illustrate the themes of Severance, such as its origin in China. The world responds by applying pressure to China, and it correlates with the idea of Western racism towards Asian Americans. This is quite similar to COVID-19 and how Asian Americans were dealt with vitriol despite having almost no basis. The only factor that led to unfair pressure was the virus's origin. Additionally, the symptoms of Shen fever are zombification through repeating past actions, and the trigger for the disease is nostalgia. This symbolizes how nostalgia can lead people to be stuck in the past, similar to the effect on zombies. The virus shows a historical context of prejudice and exploitation of Asian Americans, which was still prevalent when Severance was published.
