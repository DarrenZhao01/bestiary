Get Out Zombies

Get Out by Jordan Peele


Summary of Get Out

Get Out is Jordan Peele's film debut in horror. The movie follows Chris being invited to meet his girlfriend's parents. What seems to be a calm getaway quickly spirals into unnerving, and Chris doesn't know who is friend or foe. Spoilers: Chris realizes he is a target for surgery that would cause him to lose his mind, rendering him a zombie. He is forced to confront his girlfriend and her family to escape.

Characteristics

"Part of the desire to live in a post-racial world includes the desire not to have to talk about racism, which includes a false perception that if you are talking about race, then you're perpetuating the notion of race. I reject that." -Jordan Peele

Although not typical zombies, the victims of the brain transplants have some striking similarities. First and foremost, they lack control of their bodies, which is quite similar to the Haitian zombi. People who are a part of the surgery have their consciousness trapped in the “sunken place” in which another person controls their bodies. During Get Out, Chris' girlfriend lures him into a false sense of security, hoping to trap him in the “sunken place” forever.

Connection to its Culture

Akin to the Haitian zombi, the victims in Get Out are metaphors for slavery and discrimination in America. The surgery targeted black people for a superstitious reason and made it so their bodies would be used as tools for others, which echoes how enslaved people were treated similarly. Additionally, the victim maintains partial awareness, further cementing a feeling of fear and hopelessness. Zombies in Get Out reveal that even in the late 2010s, racial issues are still poignant in America. This gives us insight into a conflict between a perfect world and actual reality in the climate in which Get Out was created.
